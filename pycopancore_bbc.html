Mathematical Functions

1. Biodiversity-Dependent Function

The biodiversity-dependent function is given by:

$$ I = \max(0, 1 - ICC \cdot |T - z| - Id) $$

$$ H = I^\theta $$

where:

  • \( I \) is the impact of climate change and human activity on biodiversity.
  • \( ICC \) is the effect of climate change on biodiversity.
  • \( T \) is the temperature anomaly.
  • \( z \) is the response lag.
  • \( Id \) is the direct human impacts on biodiversity.
  • \( \theta \) is the exponent in the biodiversity-function relationship.

2. Terrestrial Carbon Carrying Capacity

The terrestrial carbon carrying capacity is given by:

$$ K = H \cdot \left( (1 + KC \cdot \log\left(\frac{ca}{ca_0}\right)) \cdot ct_0 \cdot QR^{\frac{T}{10}} - KA \cdot |T - z| \right) $$

where:

  • \( K \) is the terrestrial carbon carrying capacity.
  • \( H \) is the biodiversity-dependent function.
  • \( KC \) is the CO2 fertilization effect.
  • \( ca \) is the atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( ca_0 \) is the pre-industrial atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( ct_0 \) is the pre-industrial terrestrial carbon stock.
  • \( QR \) is the temperature dependence of respiration.
  • \( T \) is the temperature anomaly.
  • \( KA \) is the terrestrial carbon storage loss due to response lag.
  • \( z \) is the response lag.

3. Terrestrial Carbon Equation

The terrestrial carbon equation is given by:

$$ \frac{dct}{dt} = \frac{NPP_0}{ct_0} \cdot QR^{\frac{T}{10}} \cdot (K - ct) - LUC $$

where:

  • \( ct \) is the terrestrial carbon stock.
  • \( NPP_0 \) is the pre-industrial net primary productivity.
  • \( ct_0 \) is the pre-industrial terrestrial carbon stock.
  • \( QR \) is the temperature dependence of respiration.
  • \( T \) is the temperature anomaly.
  • \( K \) is the terrestrial carbon carrying capacity.
  • \( LUC \) is the land-use emissions.

4. Permafrost Carbon Emissions

The permafrost carbon emissions are given by:

$$ cp = pT \cdot \max(T, 0) $$

where:

  • \( cp \) is the permafrost carbon emissions.
  • \( pT \) is the permafrost thaw temperature response.
  • \( T \) is the temperature anomaly.

5. Marine Biological Pump Partial Pressure

The marine biological pump partial pressure is given by:

$$ p(cm, \Delta T) = ca_0 \left(\frac{cm}{cm_0}\right)^r \cdot \frac{1}{1 - DT \cdot T} $$

where:

  • \( p(cm, \Delta T) \) is the partial pressure of CO2 in the ocean mixed layer.
  • \( ca_0 \) is the pre-industrial atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( cm \) is the ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( cm_0 \) is the pre-industrial ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( r \) is the Revelle factor (buffer factor).
  • \( DT \) is the reduction of solubility with temperature.
  • \( T \) is the temperature anomaly.

6. Marine Biological Pump Efficiency

The marine biological pump efficiency is given by:

$$ B(0, ca_0) = 13 \cdot (1 - 0.032 \cdot 0 - BTB \cdot 0) \cdot (1 - BA \cdot (ca_0 - ca_0)) $$

$$ B(\Delta T, p(cm, \Delta T)) = 13 \cdot (1 - 0.032 \cdot T - BTB \cdot T) \cdot (1 - BA \cdot (p(cm, \Delta T) - ca_0)) $$

where:

  • \( B(0, ca_0) \) is the marine biological pump efficiency at pre-industrial conditions.
  • \( B(\Delta T, p(cm, \Delta T)) \) is the marine biological pump efficiency under changed conditions.
  • \( BTB \) is the temperature effect on the marine biological pump.
  • \( BA \) is the acidification effect on the marine biological pump.
  • \( ca_0 \) is the pre-industrial atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( cm \) is the ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( p(cm, \Delta T) \) is the partial pressure of CO2 in the ocean mixed layer.
  • \( T \) is the temperature anomaly.

7. Ocean Mixed Layer Carbon Equation

The ocean mixed layer carbon equation is given by:

$$ \frac{dcm}{dt} = \frac{D \cdot cm_0}{p(cm_0, 0)} (ca - p(cm, \Delta T)) + B(0, ca_0) - B(\Delta T, p(cm, \Delta T)) - w_0 \cdot (1 - w_T \cdot \Delta T) \cdot (cm - cm_0) $$

where:

  • \( cm \) is the ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( D \) is the atmosphere-ocean CO2 diffusion.
  • \( cm_0 \) is the pre-industrial ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( p(cm_0, 0) \) is the partial pressure of CO2 in the ocean mixed layer at pre-industrial conditions.
  • \( ca \) is the atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( p(cm, \Delta T) \) is the partial pressure of CO2 in the ocean mixed layer.
  • \( B(0, ca_0) \) is the marine biological pump efficiency at pre-industrial conditions.
  • \( B(\Delta T, p(cm, \Delta T)) \) is the marine biological pump efficiency under changed conditions.
  • \( w_0 \) is the solubility pump rate.
  • \( w_T \) is the weakening of overturning circulation with climate change.
  • \( \Delta T \) is the temperature anomaly.

8. Change in System Carbon Equation

The change in system carbon equation is given by:

$$ \frac{dcs}{dt} = e(t) - w_0 \cdot (1 - w_T \cdot \Delta T) \cdot (cm - cm_0) - (B(\Delta T, p(cm, \Delta T)) - B(0, ca_0)) $$

where:

  • \( cs \) is the change in system carbon.
  • \( e(t) \) is the emissions of fossil carbon into the atmosphere.
  • \( w_0 \) is the solubility pump rate.
  • \( w_T \) is the weakening of overturning circulation with climate change.
  • \( \Delta T \) is the temperature anomaly.
  • \( cm \) is the ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( cm_0 \) is the pre-industrial ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( B(0, ca_0) \) is the marine biological pump efficiency at pre-industrial conditions.
  • \( B(\Delta T, p(cm, \Delta T)) \) is the marine biological pump efficiency under changed conditions.

9. Atmospheric Carbon Equation

The atmospheric carbon equation based on conservation is given by:

$$ ca + ct + cm = ca_0 + ct_0 + cm_0 + cs + cp $$

$$ \frac{dca}{dt} = \frac{dcs}{dt} + ca_{conservation} - ca $$

where:

  • \( ca \) is the atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( ct \) is the terrestrial carbon stock.
  • \( cm \) is the ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( ca_0 \) is the pre-industrial atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( ct_0 \) is the pre-industrial terrestrial carbon stock.
  • \( cm_0 \) is the pre-industrial ocean mixed layer carbon stock.
  • \( cs \) is the change in system carbon.
  • \( cp \) is the permafrost carbon emissions.
  • \( ca_{conservation} = ca_0 + ct_0 + cm_0 + cs + cp - (ct + cm) \) is the conservation term.

10. Temperature Anomaly Equation

The temperature anomaly equation is given by:

$$ \frac{dT}{dt} = \frac{1}{\tau} \left( \frac{\lambda_{\text{climate}}}{\log 2} \log \left(\frac{ca}{ca_0}\right) - T \right) $$

where:

  • \( T \) is the temperature anomaly.
  • \( \tau \) is the climate lag.
  • \( \lambda_{\text{climate}} \) is the transient climate sensitivity.
  • \( ca \) is the atmospheric carbon stock.
  • \( ca_0 \) is the pre-industrial atmospheric carbon stock.
